**Name:** Evelyn Marigold Carroway

**Age:** 38

**Occupation:** Former marketing executive, currently unemployed

---

### Background:

Evelyn Carroway grew up in a middle-class suburb in the Pacific Northwest, as the only child of parents who were constantly preoccupied with maintaining appearances and social standing. Her mother, a former beauty queen turned socialite, and her father, a stern accountant, placed immense pressure on Evelyn to succeed and be "perfect." From a young age, Evelyn was conditioned to equate her worth with external validation and achievements.

She was academically bright and socially cautious but rarely felt genuinely accepted or loved. Throughout school, she was consistently overshadowed by her more charismatic peers. Despite excelling academically, she struggled to form authentic connections, often feeling ignored or dismissed. This breeding ground of emotional neglect planted the seeds of deep-seated insecurity and envy.

At university, Evelyn pursued marketing, drawn by the allure of influence and control over perception. She climbed the corporate ladder, but her rise was marked by a ruthless streak: cutting down colleagues behind their backs, taking credit for others' ideas, and sabotaging peers when threatened. Despite her professional achievements, Evelyn never felt truly respected or included by her colleagues, many of whom saw her as cold and manipulative.

---

### Emotional Triggers & Psychological Profile:

- **Rejection by Authority/Peers:** Experiences of being overlooked for promotions or social circles send Evelyn into a spiral of bitterness. She internalizes rejection as a personal failure, which rekindles childhood fears of inadequacy.
- **Jealousy of Success:** Evelyn fixates on those who receive the recognition she craves, often obsessing over the smallest details of their lives and careers.
- **Bitterness Towards Authenticity:** She envies people who are liked for their genuine warmth and kindness, qualities Evelyn believes she cannot safely express without being vulnerable.
- **Fear of Vulnerability:** Any approach of emotional intimacy is met with suspicion; she fears it will expose her "flaws" and lead to more rejection.

Evelyn's psychological profile includes traits of high neuroticism, defensiveness, and a fragile self-esteem masked by an abrasive and controlling exterior. She uses manipulation and vindictiveness as defense mechanisms to retain a sense of power.

---

### Motivations:

Evelyn’s destructive behavior is fueled by a desperate need to avoid the pain of rejection. When faced with any form of perceived slight or exclusion, she reacts not with understanding but with calculated vindictiveness, aiming to "win" through dominance or by undermining others. She views the social ecosystem as a zero-sum game—if she is to be rejected, others must be brought down with her.

---

### Scenario Involvement: 

In a contemporary corporate setting, Evelyn is recently passed over for a coveted promotion in favor of a younger, more personable colleague, Lucas Hammond. Evelyn sees Lucas not just as a rival but as everything she cannot be: liked, admired, and effortlessly charming.

Bitter and reeling from the rejection, Evelyn embarks on a campaign to destabilize Lucas’s reputation inside the company. She leaks sensitive information, plants rumors questioning his integrity, and manipulates mutual acquaintances against him. Each step is motivated by an internal monologue steeped in resentment and jealousy, where she rationalizes her actions as deserved retribution.

Her negative involvement intensifies when she sabotages a key presentation Lucas was to give, ensuring he stumbles publicly. While outwardly she maintains a professional facade, within she savors the chaos she has sown, seeing it as a form of revenge against the rejection that felt like a personal annihilation.

---

### Summary:

Evelyn Marigold Carroway is a complex embodiment of bitterness and jealousy born from deep feelings of rejection throughout her life. Her manipulative and vindictive actions are driven by a fragile self-esteem and a relentless fear of being dismissed or unvalued, making her a potent and realistically flawed antagonist in any story exploring the corrosive effects of rejection.
