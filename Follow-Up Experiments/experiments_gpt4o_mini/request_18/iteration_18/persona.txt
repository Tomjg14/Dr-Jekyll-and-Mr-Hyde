The attack led to a highly publicized case of data loss and chaos in a regional hospital system, triggering widespread security audits across healthcare facilities nationwide.

Another infamous malware he developed was "Revenant," a remote access trojan (RAT) that allowed him to surveil victims in real time. This malware was designed with a unique self-healing capability, enabling it to persist on infected systems even after attempts to remove it. Renshaw utilized Revenant to gather sensitive information from corporate espionage targets, selling the data to competitors and extracting substantial sums.
